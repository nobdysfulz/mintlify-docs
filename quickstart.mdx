By the end of this guide you'll have a live Knowledgebase help center with at least one published article organized inside a collection. The whole process takes about five minutes — no technical background required. If you'd prefer to manage content programmatically, skip to the Tip at the bottom of the page.

<Note>
  Knowledgebase offers a **14-day free trial** on all paid plans — no credit card required. You can publish your help center and invite unlimited teammates during the trial period.
</Note>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign up and create a workspace">
    Head to [app.knowledgebase.io/signup](https://app.knowledgebase.io/signup) and create your account using an email address and password, or sign in with Google or Microsoft SSO.

    After you authenticate, you'll land on the **New Workspace** screen:

    1. Enter a **Workspace name** (e.g., "Acme Help Center").
    2. Confirm or edit the auto-generated **URL slug** — this becomes `app.knowledgebase.io/<slug>`.
    3. Click **Create workspace**.

    You'll be redirected to your workspace dashboard, which is empty and ready for your first article.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create your first article">
    In the left sidebar, click **New Article** (or press `C` anywhere in the dashboard).

    The rich-text editor opens with three areas to fill in:

    | Field | What to do |
    |---|---|
    | **Title** | Write a clear, specific title (e.g., "How to reset your password"). |
    | **Body** | Draft your content using headings, bullet lists, images, and code blocks. Markdown shortcuts like `**bold**` and `# Heading` work natively. |
    | **Tags** | Add one or more tags (e.g., `account`, `security`) to improve search relevance. |

    Click **Save draft** in the top-right corner when you're done. Your article is saved but not yet visible to readers.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Organize into a collection">
    Collections are folders that give your help center structure. Create one before publishing so your article has a home.

    **Option A — From the sidebar:**
    1. Click **Collections** in the left sidebar, then **New Collection**.
    2. Give the collection a name (e.g., "Getting Started") and choose an emoji or icon.
    3. Drag your article from the **Drafts** list and drop it into the new collection.

    **Option B — From the article settings:**
    1. Open your article and click the **⚙ Settings** icon in the top-right toolbar.
    2. Under **Collection**, select your collection from the dropdown.
    3. Click **Save**.

    <Tip>
      You can nest collections up to three levels deep. Use top-level collections for broad topics (e.g., "Account & Billing") and sub-collections for specifics (e.g., "Invoices").
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Publish your help center">
    You're ready to go live. Follow these steps to publish:

    1. Open **Workspace Settings** (gear icon in the bottom-left corner).
    2. Navigate to the **Publishing** tab.
    3. Toggle **Help center visibility** from **Private** to **Public**.
    4. Copy the public URL shown below the toggle (e.g., `https://acme.knowledgebase.io`) and share it with your customers.

    To publish the individual article you created, open it in the editor and click **Publish** in the top-right corner. Published articles are immediately visible to anyone with access to your help center.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Next Steps

You've got a live help center — here's where to go from here.

<Tip>
  Want to create or update articles without opening the editor? Use the [Knowledgebase REST API](/api/introduction) to manage content programmatically, bulk-import existing documentation, or automate content workflows.
</Tip>

- **Invite your team** — Go to **Workspace Settings → Members** to add editors and admins. See the [Account Setup guide](/account-setup) for details.
- **Customize your domain** — Map `help.yourcompany.com` to your workspace in **Settings → Custom Domain**.
- **Explore integrations** — Connect Knowledgebase to Slack, Intercom, or Zendesk in **Settings → Integrations**.
