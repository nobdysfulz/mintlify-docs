Your help center is often the first place a customer goes when something goes wrong — and it should feel like a seamless extension of your product, not a generic third-party page. Knowledgebase gives you full control over your help center's visual identity, from logo and colors to layout, navigation, and custom CSS, so you can deliver a consistent brand experience without writing any code.

---

## Logo and favicon

Upload your logo and favicon from **Settings → Branding** in your dashboard.

- **Logo** — displayed in the top-left corner of your help center header. Upload a PNG, SVG, or WebP file. For best results, use a horizontal logo at least 200 px wide with a transparent background so it renders cleanly on both light and dark backgrounds.
- **Favicon** — the small icon that appears in the browser tab. Upload a 32×32 or 64×64 PNG or ICO file. If you do not upload a favicon, Knowledgebase uses a default icon.

Changes take effect immediately after saving — no redeployment required.

## Brand colors

In **Settings → Branding → Colors**, use the color picker to set your **Primary Color**. This single color controls the visual accent throughout your help center, including:

- Button backgrounds (e.g., the search button and CTA links)
- Link hover states
- Active states in the navigation menu
- Highlighted text matches in search results
- The progress indicator at the top of the page while articles load

Enter a hex value directly (e.g., `#5B21B6`) or use the visual color picker to find the right shade. Choose a color with sufficient contrast against white backgrounds to keep your help center accessible.

## Homepage layout

Customize your help center homepage from **Settings → Layout → Homepage**.

<Accordion title="Hero section">
  The hero is the banner at the top of your homepage containing the search bar. You can customize:
  - **Headline text** — the large heading above the search bar (e.g., *How can we help you?*).
  - **Subheadline text** — a supporting line beneath the headline.
  - **Background color or image** — choose a solid color, a gradient, or upload a background image.
  Toggle the hero off entirely if you prefer a more compact homepage that leads directly with your collections.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Featured collections">
  Choose up to 6 collections to feature prominently on the homepage as large cards. Featured collections appear above the full collection grid. Drag them into your preferred order using the ⠿ handle.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Show/hide sections">
  Use the toggle switches on the Layout page to show or hide individual homepage sections:
  - **Hero section**
  - **Featured collections**
  - **Full collection grid**
  - **Recently updated articles**
  - **Contact support CTA** (links to an email address or external support URL you specify)
</Accordion>

## Navigation and footer

**Custom navigation links:** Go to **Settings → Navigation** to add links to your help center's top navigation bar. Each link has a **label** and a **URL** — you can point to internal help center pages or external URLs (e.g., your product app, status page, or community forum).

**Footer text:** Add a custom footer message in **Settings → Footer**. This appears at the bottom of every help center page. Use it for copyright notices, disclaimers, or a short tagline.

**Social links:** In the same Footer settings panel, add links to your social profiles (Twitter/X, LinkedIn, GitHub, etc.). Knowledgebase renders them as icon buttons in the footer automatically.

## Custom CSS

For complete design control, add custom CSS in **Settings → Advanced → Custom CSS**. Styles you enter here are injected into every page of your help center and override the default theme.

For example, to change the header background color and text color:

```css
.kb-header {
  background-color: #1a1a2e;
  color: #ffffff;
}
```

You can target any element in your help center using the browser's developer tools to inspect class names. Common customization targets include `.kb-header`, `.kb-footer`, `.kb-article-body`, `.kb-search-input`, and `.kb-collection-card`.

<Warning>
  Custom CSS is applied on top of Knowledgebase's default styles, which may change between platform updates. After a Knowledgebase update, review your custom CSS to make sure selectors still match the intended elements. Breaking changes to the default theme are announced in the [Knowledgebase changelog](https://knowledgebase.io/changelog), but it is your responsibility to maintain any custom overrides you have added.
</Warning>

<Tip>
  Want to serve your help center from your own domain (e.g., `help.yourcompany.com`) to complete the branded experience? See the [Custom domain setup guide](/guides/publishing#custom-domain-setup) for step-by-step DNS configuration instructions.
</Tip>
