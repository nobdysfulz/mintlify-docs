A well-organized knowledge base helps customers find answers on their own and reduces support volume. Knowledgebase gives you two primary tools for structure: **collections**, which act as folders that group related articles, and **tags**, which create flexible cross-cutting links between articles regardless of where they live. Using both together gives your readers multiple natural paths to the content they need.

---

## Creating a collection

Collections are the top-level containers in your knowledge base. Think of them as chapters or categories — for example, *Getting Started*, *Billing*, or *Integrations*.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Collections → New Collection">
    In your Knowledgebase dashboard, click **Collections** in the left sidebar, then select **New Collection** in the top-right corner.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a name, description, and icon">
    Fill in the following fields:
    - **Name** — a short, clear label your customers will see (e.g., *Account & Billing*).
    - **Description** — one or two sentences explaining what articles this collection contains. This text appears on your help center homepage below the collection name.
    - **Icon** — choose an emoji or upload a custom image to give the collection visual identity in your help center layout.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set visibility">
    Choose who can see this collection:
    - **Public** — visible to all visitors of your help center.
    - **Private** — visible only to logged-in workspace members; customers cannot see it.
    - **Password-protected** — visible to anyone who enters the collection password. See [Publishing](/guides/publishing) for details.

    Click **Create Collection** to save.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Adding articles to collections

You can assign articles to collections in two ways:

**From the article editor:** Open any article, scroll to the **Article Settings** panel on the right, and click the **Collection** dropdown. Select the collection you want — the article is added immediately.

**By drag and drop:** Open the **Collections** view in your dashboard. Drag any article card from the *Uncategorized* list or from another collection and drop it onto the target collection name in the sidebar. A blue highlight indicates a valid drop target.

An article can belong to only one collection at a time. To move an article, drag it to the new collection or update the **Collection** field in its settings panel.

---

## Nesting collections

For large knowledge bases, you can create subcollections to add a second or third level of hierarchy — for example, *Integrations → CRM → Salesforce*.

To **create a subcollection**:
1. Open the parent collection from the **Collections** sidebar.
2. Click **+ Add Subcollection** inside the collection detail view.
3. Give it a name, description, and icon, then click **Create**.

Knowledgebase supports up to **3 levels of nesting** (collection → subcollection → sub-subcollection). Deeper nesting is not available to keep navigation from becoming too complex for readers.

To **reorder collections or subcollections**, drag them by the ⠿ handle in the Collections sidebar. The order you set here is the order customers see on your help center homepage and in the navigation menu.

## Using tags

Tags are lightweight labels you can attach to any article. Unlike collections, a single article can have multiple tags, and tags are not hierarchical — they work more like keywords.

**Tagging an article:**
Open the article editor, navigate to **Article Settings → Tags**, and type a tag name. Press **Enter** to add it. You can add as many tags as you like. Existing tags appear as suggestions as you type.

**Browsing by tag:**
In your help center, clicking a tag on any article shows a filtered list of all published articles that share that tag — giving readers a quick way to explore related content.

**Managing tags:**
Go to **Settings → Tags** in your dashboard to rename, merge, or delete tags across your entire knowledge base. Merging tags is useful when you have near-duplicates (e.g., *setup* and *set-up*) — the merge replaces all instances of one tag with the other.

---

<Tip>
  Keep your top-level collections broad and limited in number — aim for 5 to 8 collections maximum. Readers scan the help center homepage quickly, and too many top-level categories create decision fatigue. Use subcollections and tags to add granularity beneath those broad buckets.
</Tip>
